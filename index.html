Spectre V1 - Analysis and Demonstration

Cybersecurity Research Educational Project 2024

Spectre V1 is a flaw in modern processors that comes from the way they speed things up by guessing what will happen next. Sometimes these guesses briefly touch information they shouldnâ€™t, and small traces of that information can be measured and reconstructed in a controlled experiment.

Our project explains how this happens and demonstrates it safely, showing how a performance feature can accidentally create a security risk. It highlights why improving processor design is important for keeping future systems secure.
